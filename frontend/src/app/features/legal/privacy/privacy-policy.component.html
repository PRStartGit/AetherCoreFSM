Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us

Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Last Updated: January 1, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to Zynthio ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your personal information and your right to privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our digital food safety management platform.

Zynthio is operated by Zynthio Ltd, located at Edinburgh, Scotland, UK. We provide a web-based platform for restaurants, pubs, and hospitality businesses to manage food safety compliance, checklists, and inspections.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide to Us

  • Account Information: Name, email address, phone number, business name, and job title when you register for an account
  • Business Information: Business address, number of sites, industry type, and compliance requirements
  • Payment Information: Billing details and payment card information (processed securely through our payment provider)
  • Task Completion Data: Records of completed checklists, temperature readings, photos, notes, and timestamps
  • Communication Data: Messages, support requests, and correspondence with our team

2.2 Information Automatically Collected

  • Device Information: IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, features used, time spent on platform, click patterns
  • Location Data: General location based on IP address (not precise GPS location)
  • Cookies and Tracking: See our Cookie Policy for detailed information

2.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Payment processing data from GoCardless (Direct Debit and bank payments)
  • Authentication data if you sign in via third-party services

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following purposes:

  • Service Delivery: To provide, maintain, and improve the Zynthio platform
  • Account Management: To create and manage your user account
  • Compliance Tracking: To store and display food safety records, temperature logs, and inspection data
  • Communication: To send service updates, security alerts, and support messages
  • Marketing: To send promotional emails about new features (you can opt-out anytime)
  • Analytics: To understand how users interact with our platform and improve user experience
  • Security: To detect, prevent, and address fraud, security issues, and technical problems
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes
  • Notifications: To send task reminders, alerts, and updates via email and browser notifications (if enabled)

4. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

If you are located in the UK or European Economic Area (EEA), we process your personal data based on:

  • Contract Performance: Processing necessary to provide our services to you
  • Legitimate Interests: To improve our services, prevent fraud, and ensure security
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with UK and EU data protection laws
  • Consent: For marketing communications and optional features (you can withdraw consent anytime)

5. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

5.1 Service Providers

  • Cloud hosting providers (e.g., AWS, DigitalOcean)
  • Payment processors (e.g., GoCardless)
  • Email service providers (e.g., SendGrid, Mailgun)
  • Analytics providers (e.g., Google Analytics)

5.2 Business Transfers

If Zynthio is acquired, merged, or sold, your information may be transferred to the new entity.

5.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or to protect the rights and safety of Zynthio and our users.

5.4 With Your Consent

We may share information with third parties when you explicitly consent to such sharing.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to provide our services and comply with legal obligations:

  • Account Data: Retained while your account is active and for 7 years after closure (for compliance purposes)
  • Food Safety Records: Retained for 7 years to comply with UK Food Safety regulations
  • Payment Records: Retained for 7 years for tax and accounting purposes
  • Marketing Data: Retained until you opt-out or 2 years of inactivity

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for data in transit
  • Encryption of sensitive data at rest
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Access controls and authentication requirements
  • Secure backup and disaster recovery procedures

However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Your Rights and Choices

8.1 GDPR Rights (UK/EEA Users)

You have the following rights under GDPR:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to Restriction: Limit how we use your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Object: Object to certain types of processing
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time

8.2 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at hello@zynthio.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days.

8.3 Marketing Communications

You can opt-out of marketing emails by clicking "unsubscribe" in any marketing email or by contacting us directly.

9. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience. Please see our Cookie Policy for detailed information about the cookies we use and how to manage them.

10. Third-Party Links

Our platform may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies.

11. Children's Privacy

Zynthio is not intended for users under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact us immediately.

12. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries outside the UK/EEA. When we transfer data internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as:

  • EU Standard Contractual Clauses
  • Adequacy decisions by the European Commission
  • Other legally approved transfer mechanisms

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by email or by posting a notice on our platform. Your continued use of Zynthio after changes become effective constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
